# Deck profiles

Pick exactly one *primary* profile before you design. The profile decides which
proof objects are mandatory and which failure modes are disqualifying — it sits
above any visual score. Add secondary profiles as extra constraints in the claim
spine when more than one applies.

## finance / IR
Earnings, board, investor, operating-review, and metrics decks.
- **Mandatory:** a KPI summary with units and period; a revenue/mix table or
  bridge; a margin or cash profile; the relevant retention/backlog/outlook proof.
- **Rules:** only reported figures or clearly-labelled calculations from them;
  keep units, fiscal periods, and GAAP/non-GAAP labels visible; bridges must
  reconcile numerically; combination charts must make their scale logic explicit.
- **Fails on:** a pretty chart with unsupported values, a generic "growth is
  strong" title, two metrics sharing one axis for no reason, missing footnotes.

## product / platform
SaaS, product, platform, workflow, and ecosystem narratives.
- **Mandatory:** a platform/architecture map; a use-case or journey visual; an
  adoption/expansion signal; a product-to-business linkage slide.
- **Rules:** show the system, not a feature list; tie each capability to
  adoption, expansion, efficiency, or revenue; diagram labels must be concrete
  enough that the diagram would be wrong on another company.
- **Fails on:** repeated feature-card grids, vague boxes ("AI", "Data"),
  product slides disconnected from outcomes, arrows that float.

## gtm / growth
Go-to-market, marketing, growth, subscription, and commercialization decks.
- **Mandatory:** a visible growth loop or funnel; a segment or cohort proof; a
  monetization/value bridge; an engagement or retention trend.
- **Rules:** connect reach → activation → engagement → monetization → retention;
  express brand through colour, type, and layout — never a fabricated mark.
- **Fails on:** funnel labels with no movement between stages, brand parody,
  marketing claims without quantified proof.

## engineering / platform
Developer, AI, infrastructure, data, and security decks.
- **Mandatory:** accurate system diagrams; technical labels that survive
  executive simplification; metrics tied to the architecture.
- **Fails on:** technically vague diagrams, labels stripped of meaning, developer
  detail that drowns the executive story.

## strategy / leadership
Investor-day, board, transformation, and market-strategy decks.
- **Mandatory:** chapter discipline; a market frame that returns in the operating
  plan; explicit strategic bets; transition slides that carry the thesis.
- **Fails on:** chapter dividers without thesis movement, a market frame that
  never reappears.

## consumer / image-led
Lookbooks, brand, campaign, lifestyle, and any deck where the audience needs to
visually inspect the subject.
- **Mandatory:** real assets or explicit asset provenance; editorial hierarchy;
  client-ready copy.
- **Fails on:** stock-looking imagery, weak crops, unverified provenance, generic
  outreach copy.

## appendix-heavy
Dense tables, disclosures, and source packs.
- **Mandatory:** an index / page markers; readable small-type thresholds;
  consistent table grammar; explicit source-density rules.
- **Fails on:** unreadable tables, missing index, density that hides the answer.

## Brand-authenticity gate (applies to every profile)
Treat logos, mascots, app icons, product UI, and partner/customer marks as
identity assets. Do **not** draw, trace, or approximate an official mark. Use a
verified asset with provenance, a user-supplied asset, or none — and lean on
colour, type, layout, and sourced metrics for brand instead. A fabricated or
approximated official mark fails the deck regardless of visual score.
